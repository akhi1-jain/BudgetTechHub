How to Choose the Right Camera Under ₹1 Lakh

Before you decide which camera to buy, it is important to understand a few basic things. These points will help you choose the right camera based on what you need.

1. Mirrorless vs DSLR

  • Mirrorless: Smaller, lighter, better video features, faster autofocus (perfect for travel, vlogging, and everyday use).
  • DSLR: Bigger, more rugged build, better battery life, and an optical viewfinder (preferred by traditional photographers).

2. Lens Compatibility

Different brands use different lens mounts. Always check lens availability and cost before committing to a system:

  • Sony: Uses the E-mount (wide range of third-party lenses like Sigma and Tamron).
  • Canon Mirrorless: Uses RF-S and RF mounts (a growing, modern selection).
  • Nikon Mirrorless: Uses the Z-mount (growing lens support).
  • DSLR (Canon EF/Nikon F): Huge collection of affordable, new, and used lenses available.

3. Video or Photography?

  • For Video/Vlogging: Look for 4K recording, a flip screen, good autofocus (AF), and a mic input. Cameras like the Sony ZV-E10 II and Canon EOS R50 are specialized here.
  • For Photography: Focus on image quality, fast burst shooting, and strong low-light performance. A larger sensor (like APS-C or full-frame) is usually better.

4. Upgradability

Mirrorless systems are generally more future-proof. Starting with an APS-C mirrorless allows you to upgrade to a full-frame body later while often keeping your existing lenses (with limitations). DSLR lines are receiving fewer updates from manufacturers.
